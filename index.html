A Travel Guide To Different Cities

Choosing where to go when you travel is a difficult decision to make. You have to consider what they have to see, what there is to do, how to be a safe traveler in that city, and look at costs! In my travel blog, I go over a few cities that I have personally been to and a few things you should know if you are looking to travel there. Stay tuned for new reviews on places I have traveled!

Rio De Janeiro

Rio De Janeiro is a beautiful city in Brazil. It has one of the 7 wonders of the world, Christ the Redeemer. They also have the best beaches, Copacabana beach being the most famous. There is a lot to do and see. However, I personally did not enjoy the food in any of the parts in Brazil as I thought they were pretty bland. As for walking around to sightsee, it may be dangerous as people tend to pickpocket belongings, so definitely stay with a large group or have a designated tour bus. In Brazil, in all three cities I have been, nobody speaks English. It was very hard with translation, even in the airport and airplane going there, so I also reccomend having a tour guide that is fluent in Portuguese.

The Hamptons

The Hamptons is a really good place for a getaway if you aren't looking to explore and are just looking to relax. It is good for a small family or group of friends as there isn't too much to do, but it being the lavish part of New York is really calming. It's really interesting to be in the Hamptons after being in New York city as they are complete opposites. Everything in the Hamptons is very expensive as there are no franchises at all. So if you are looking for a McDonald's or a Walmart, all you are going to find are bakeries and fresh/organic grocery stores. The beaches are really nice an clean. In fact, Coopers Beach is one of the top 10 best beaches in America. The restaurants in the Hamptons have very delectable food, but are pretty expensive. Staying in an AirBnB here was a good decision in my opinion as we were able to explore around the neighborhoods freely, finding access to a private beach! Overall, I really liked the calm aspect of the Hamptons and it makes for the perfect girls trip!
